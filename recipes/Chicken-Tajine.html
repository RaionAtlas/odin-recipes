Moroccan Chicken Tajine

Description

Moroccan Chicken Tagine is a traditional North African dish simmered to perfection.

Tender chicken thighs are infused with warm spices like turmeric, cinnamon, and paprika, then slow-cooked with onions, preserved lemon, and a mix of green and black olives.

  • Servings: 4
  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 35 minutes
  • Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 preserved lemon, sliced (or zest of 1 lemon if unavailable)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • ½ cup green olives (or a mix of green and black)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Season and Brown the Chicken: Season chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and half of the spices. In a large skillet or tagine pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken skin-side down for 5–7 minutes until golden. Flip and cook the other side for 3 minutes. Remove and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pan, add onions and garlic. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in remaining spices and cook for 1 minute.

  3. Simmer the Tagine: Return the chicken to the pan. Add preserved lemon slices and olives. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook on low for 25 minutes until chicken is tender and flavors meld.

  4. Finish and Serve: Uncover and let the sauce reduce slightly if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro and serve with couscous or crusty bread.
