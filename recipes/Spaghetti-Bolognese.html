Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

A hearty bowl of classic spaghetti Bolognese, featuring perfectly cooked pasta tossed in a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce and topped with freshly grated Parmesan.

Comfort food at its finest — simple, satisfying, and full of flavor.
  • Servings: 4
  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 20 minutes
  • Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped (optional)
  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef
  • 400g (14 oz) canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Steps

  1. Sauté the vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, grated carrot, and celery (if using). Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

  2. Cook the beef: Add the ground beef to the pan. Cook, breaking it apart with a spoon, until browned and no longer pink.

  3. Add tomatoes and seasoning: Stir in the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Cook the spaghetti: While the sauce simmers, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  5. Combine and serve: Toss the cooked spaghetti with the Bolognese sauce until well combined. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
